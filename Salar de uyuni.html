Welcome to Salar de Uyuni

Salar de Uyuni, located in southwest Bolivia, is the world's largest salt flat, spanning over 10,000 square kilometers. Formed from prehistoric evaporated lakes, it offers visitors an unparalleled sense of scale and beauty.

Why You Should Visit

  • Experience the surreal mirror effect during the rainy season.
  • Witness breathtaking sunsets that blend the earth and sky.
  • Explore unique salt hotels and the nearby Eduardo Avaroa National Park.

Visitor Information

Location: Southwestern Bolivia, near Uyuni town.
Best Time to Visit: November to March for reflections; April to October for dry geometric patterns.
Tips: Wear sunglasses to protect your eyes from the reflective salt, and bring plenty of sunscreen.

Explore the geological wonder of the Eye of the Sahara!

Welcome to the Richat Structure

The Richat Structure, also called the "Eye of the Sahara," is a prominent circular formation located in the Sahara Desert of Mauritania. Spanning over 40 kilometers in diameter, this natural wonder is a unique geological phenomenon that can be seen clearly from space.

What Makes It Unique?

  • A striking circular shape believed to be caused by erosion of layered sedimentary rock.
  • Once thought to be an impact crater but later identified as a geologic dome.
  • Visible from space, making it a fascinating feature for astronauts and researchers.

Visitor Information

Location: Sahara Desert, near Ouadane, Mauritania.
Best Time to Visit: November to February, for cooler desert temperatures.
Tips: Access is challenging; plan with local guides for a safe and informative visit.
